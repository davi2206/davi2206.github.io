About a month ago, I posted a challenge on this site, stating that I would walk for at least 10 minutes, every day for 30 days. Now those 30 days have passed, and this is my conclusion on the challenge. 

## Surprisingly consistent
When I started the challenge, I was hoping to do all 30 days, but I was fully expecting to miss at least a few along the way. Either from forgetting to get it done, or from lazynes or the weather being so bad I decided to stay in. <br>
Now, after 30 days, I can proudly say, I ended up doing all 30 walks. Most were around 15 minutes. Some were only just over 10 minutes. The longest walk was about 45 minutes. All in all, pretty solid work. 

## Cheats?
Although I have counted all 30 days, there have been a few days that are only just making the cut. <br>
One day I came home really late, after a boardgame night at work and when I remembered to do the walk, it was just passed midnight. Because of this, the walk was technically the next day, but I counted it still, because it was still the same night, and I did the walk for the next day, after a good nights sleep. <br>
Another day, I was at a zoo with family. I didn't specifically do a 10 minute walk that day, but we were walking around the park for something like 5 hours. However, it is a very different type of walking, when being in a park like that, and looking at animals, compared to taking a purposeful exercice walk. I still counted the day as complete, but I was always a little 50/50 on weather it should really count or not. <br>
Feel free to share your opinion on weather these days should have counted towards the challenge, or if they were 'cheats'. 

## Pledge
As a stronger motivation, I promised to give {% money 1000 %} to charity, for each day I missed. Since I didn't miss any days, I don't owe anyone anythnig. That honestly makes me feel a little bad, since I was expecting to miss a few days, so getting a perfect run almost feels like cheating the charities out of their money. <br>
Because of that, and because of the few days that were only just on the edge of being completed, I have decided to give {% money 2000 %} to Children's Cancer Foundation, just to make sure the slate is cleared.
